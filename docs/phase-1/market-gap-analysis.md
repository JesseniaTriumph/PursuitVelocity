# Market Gap Analysis

---

## The Gap in Plain Language

Every major platform gives developers a place to **store** their work (GitHub) or **list** their credentials (LinkedIn). None give early-career developers from intensive programs a place to **discover, connect, and ship together** — with intelligence behind the matching.

The gap is not a lack of tools. It's a lack of tools built *for this specific person at this specific moment in their career*.

---

## Market Sizing

### Total Addressable Market (TAM)
- US coding bootcamp graduates (2024): ~90,000/year
- International + self-taught community: ~2M developers in early career
- **TAM: ~2M users globally**

### Serviceable Addressable Market (SAM)
- US bootcamp grads at programs with community infrastructure needs: ~30,000/year
- Programs similar to Pursuit (equity-focused, intensive): ~20 programs nationally
- **SAM: ~6,000 new users/year**

### Serviceable Obtainable Market (SOM — Year 1)
- Pursuit network: 2,000 fellows + alumni
- 2 partner programs: +1,000 users
- **SOM: ~3,000 users**

## Placement Strategy

**Positioning statement:**
> Velocity is the only community platform built specifically for Pursuit fellows — giving every builder a public identity, an AI matching engine, and the infrastructure to ship together.

**Positioning tier:** Premium community tool. Not a job board. Not social media. Not a portfolio site. Something new.

---

## Go-to-Market Wedge

1. **Mandate within Pursuit cohort** — not optional, part of the program
2. **Staff seed the feed** — program coordinators post events, tutorials
3. **Alumni network** — 2,000 existing people who want to give back
4. **External Lookbook links** — every builder's Lookbook is public, indexed by Google
5. **Employer word-of-mouth** — employers find talent, tell other employers
